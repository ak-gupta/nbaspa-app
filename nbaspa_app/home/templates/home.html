Welcome!

Player impact metrics are supposed to help us understand one thing: how much does a player impact winning?

To answer this question, we used play-by-play data to quantify the impact of any given play in terms of how it changes the probability of a team winning the game. We won't go into detail here (this documentation has all the information you need), but we'll lay out the major benefits and limitationsof our impact metric.

The metric is highly contextual.

The metric can capture the effects of out-of-game factors like team quality and fatigue as well as the time and score of the game. While some might view this as a limitation, we think that contextual information can provide us with more information about the impact that a player is adding beyond the baseline expectation for the team. However, we've added toggles in the player comparison visualizations to remove the context where desired.

A lot of impactful basketball is not captured in play-by-play data.

Especially defense. Rotations, ball denial, and point-of-attack defense are great examples of impactful plays you won't find on traditional boxscores or in eventlogs. We would love to attribute credit to players for contesting shots, but that information is not available to us on a play-by-play basis (see here if you want to know more about our underlying data).
